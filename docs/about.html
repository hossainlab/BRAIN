About BRAIN

Course Information & Team

About the Course

Bioinformatics Research with AI in Neurosciences (BRAIN) is a comprehensive research-oriented training program designed to bridge the gap between neuroscience, bioinformatics, and artificial intelligence.

Mission

Our mission is to empower researchers, students, and professionals with cutting-edge computational skills to analyze complex neurogenomics data and apply AI methods to advance neuroscience research.

Vision

We envision a future where computational neuroscience and AI-driven approaches are integral to understanding brain function, disease mechanisms, and developing novel therapeutic strategies.

Course Objectives

By the end of this training program, participants will be able to:

  • Understand the fundamentals of neurogenomics and single-cell technologies
  • Navigate and utilize Linux command-line tools for bioinformatics
  • Program in Python for data analysis and visualization
  • Process and analyze single-cell RNA-seq data from raw data to biological insights
  • Apply quality control, dimensionality reduction, and clustering methods
  • Perform differential expression and cell type annotation
  • Conduct trajectory inference and spatial transcriptomics analyses
  • Implement deep learning methods for single-cell genomics
  • Integrate AI approaches into neuroscience research workflows

Target Audience

This course is designed for:

  • Graduate students in neuroscience, bioinformatics, or computational biology
  • Postdoctoral researchers working with neurogenomics data
  • Faculty members looking to expand their computational skills
  • Bioinformaticians interested in neuroscience applications
  • Data scientists transitioning to biological research
  • Medical researchers studying neurological disorders

Prerequisites

ImportantWhat You Need

Required: - Basic understanding of molecular biology and genetics - Access to a computer (Windows, Mac, or Linux) - Willingness to learn programming and command-line tools

Helpful but not required: - Prior experience with Python or R - Familiarity with Linux/Unix command line - Background in statistics or data analysis

Course Philosophy

Hands-On Learning

We believe in learning by doing. Every module includes practical exercises with real-world datasets.

Research-Oriented

Course materials are based on current research practices and cutting-edge methods used in leading neuroscience laboratories.

Open Science

We promote open-source tools, reproducible research practices, and collaborative science.

Supportive Environment

We foster a welcoming learning environment where questions are encouraged and mistakes are learning opportunities.

Technical Requirements

Software & Tools

Throughout the course, you will work with:

  • Operating System: Linux/Unix (or WSL for Windows users)
  • Programming Languages: Python (primary), bash scripting
  • Key Tools:
    • CellRanger (10x Genomics)
    • Scanpy (Python single-cell analysis)
    • PyTorch/TensorFlow (deep learning)
    • Jupyter Notebooks
    • Git/GitHub

Hardware Requirements

  • Minimum: 8GB RAM, 50GB free disk space
  • Recommended: 16GB+ RAM, 100GB+ free disk space
  • Note: Some computationally intensive modules may benefit from access to HPC resources

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this course suitable for beginners?

Yes! While some modules are advanced, we start with foundations and build progressively. No prior programming experience is required, though it is helpful.

What is the time commitment?

Each module requires 2-4 hours for lectures and tutorials, plus 2-3 hours for self-study and practice. Total time commitment is approximately 60-80 hours over 8 weeks.

Will I get a certificate?

Yes, participants who complete all modules and meet the requirements will receive a certificate of completion.

Can I take individual modules?

The course is designed as a comprehensive program with sequential modules. However, some flexibility may be available depending on your background and goals.

What datasets will we use?

We use publicly available neuroscience datasets, including single-cell RNA-seq data from brain tissues, neurological disease models, and developmental studies.
